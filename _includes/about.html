About

I am a research associate at the Complutense University of Madrid (UCM), where my work focuses on developing artificial intelligence models to study collective learning and collective intelligence. By combining reinforcement learning and neural network approaches, I aim to better understand how collective behavior dynamics emerge in humans and other biological systems. I am a member of the Interdisciplinary Group of Complex Systems (GISC) at UCM and collaborate on the project HFSP – The Emergence of Collective Intelligence: Understanding Human Behavior through AI Agents, coordinated by Prof. Miguel Ruiz García.

I hold a dual degree in Mathematics and Physics from UCM, including a one-year academic exchange at the University of Geneva, and a MSc in Artificial Intelligence from Alfonso X El Sabio University.

Beyond artificial intelligence, I am deeply interested in mathematical modeling, data analysis, and their applications across diverse areas—from time series forecasting to process optimization. Outside of research, I enjoy exploring history, politics, and finance.
