About

As a researcher in training at the Complutense University of Madrid (UCM), my work focuses on developing artificial intelligence models applied to collective learning and collective intelligence, combining reinforcement learning and neural network approaches, aiming to understand how collective behavior dynamics emerge in humans and other biological systems. I am part of the Interdisciplinary Group of Complex Systems (GISC) at UCM and the project HFSP - The Emergence of Collective Intelligence: Understanding Human Behavior through AI Agents, coordinated by Dr. Miguel Ruiz García.

I hold a degree in Mathematics and Physics from Complutense University of Madrid, with a one-year exchange at the University of Geneva, and I am currently pursuing a Master's in Artificial Intelligence at Alfonso X El Sabio University.

Besides my work in artificial intelligence, I have a strong interest in mathematical modeling, data analysis, and their applications in various fields, from time series forecasting to process optimization. In my free time, I enjoy exploring topics in history, politics, and finance.
